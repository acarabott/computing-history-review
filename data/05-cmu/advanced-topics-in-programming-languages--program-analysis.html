Description:

SECTION X: DESCRIPTION: This seminar provides an overview of domain-specific languages. We begin with a survey of domain-specific languages, from those well-specified in research to those popular in practice, as well as tools and techniques for specifying and implementing domain-specific languages. The course will have more in-depth units, one on languages for security and another on languages for biological modelling. Students will emerge with an understanding of what it means to be a domain-specific language, how to formally specify and prove properties about domain-specific languages, and implementation techniques for domain-specific languages. Prerequisites: Students should have strong backgrounds in programming and formal semantics of programming languages. Text: There will be no text but there will be many research papers distributed throughout the course. Method of evaluation: Grading will be based on reading responses, presentation of research papers, and a final project.
