Description:

Description: 15-745 is a graduate course in optimizing compilers for modern architectures. The learning objectives are: To understand the salient techniques used in building optimizing compilers that effectively exploit modern processor architectures. To gain hands-on experience in implementing significant optimizations within the framework of a modern research compiler. To investigate novel ideas in optimizing compilers through a six-week long research project.
